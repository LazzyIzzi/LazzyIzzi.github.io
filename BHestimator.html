Beam Hardening Estimator                                            Home

 

Beam hardening is the shifting of the broad spectrum of a conventional x-ray source toward higher average (hard) energies as it passes through filters, samples and detectors. This results in an over-estimate of attenuation in thinner portions of the sample.  It produces the well-known cupping artifact in tomographic images.

The ImageJ Beam Hardening Estimator plugin is built upon the Mu Mass Calculator library.  It provides an interactive way to estimate the severity of beam hardening artifact when acquiring radiographs or tomography data.

The figure shows the Dialog Box(left), a schematic of a simple radiography or CT scanner layout(top), a plot of intensity vs X-ray energy at five sampling points along the beam path(middle), and the report issued for each trial configuration(bottom). In the schematic, the arrow colors correspond to the plot colors, the arrow widths indicate spectral width and the length indicates intensity.

The goal of this plugin is to quickly optimize the source, filter, and detector configuration to obtain good contrast and high count rates.  A user makes appropriate selections in the non-modal dialog and clicks the Update Plot button.  The spectral dependencies are displayed and the report is updated with the configuration and beam hardening metrics obtained from detected intensities with and without the specimen in the x-ray beam.

Simple thresholding of tomographic reconstructions typically requires less than 5% beam hardening over the entire image for successful segmentation of components.  This may vary depending on materials contrast.

Special Note:  This is NOT a detective quantum efficiency (DQE) calculator, the detector is modeled as ideal with the exception of a correction for photons that pass through the scintillator without being detected. Real detectors vary considerably in design, construction and response.
